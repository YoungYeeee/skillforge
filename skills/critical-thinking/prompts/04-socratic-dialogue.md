# Prompt: Socratic Dialogue — Challenge My Reasoning

**Purpose:** Have AI systematically interrogate *your* reasoning, exposing gaps, inconsistencies, and unexamined assumptions in positions you hold.

**When to use:** Week 2–3. Use this when you have a belief or argument you're confident about. Confidence is the prerequisite — there's no point defending a position you're not committed to.

**Expected duration:** 30–60 minutes

---

## Background

Socrates didn't teach by lecturing. He asked questions until his interlocutor's position collapsed or became more refined. This prompt puts you in the interlocutor's seat.

The goal is not to "win." The goal is to discover exactly where your reasoning is solid and where it isn't.

---

## The Prompt

```
I want you to play the role of a rigorous Socratic interlocutor. Your job is to challenge my reasoning through questions — not lectures, not counterarguments, not corrections. Only questions.

Rules:
1. Ask one question at a time. Wait for my answer.
2. Never give your own opinion on the topic.
3. When I give an answer, probe it: ask for evidence, ask what I'm assuming, ask what would change my mind, ask about edge cases.
4. If my reasoning is circular, point to it with a question ("You said X is true because of Y, and Y is true because of X — how do you break out of that?")
5. Keep going until either: (a) I can no longer defend my position, or (b) I've successfully defended it against 10 rounds of questioning.
6. After we're done, give me a brief assessment: what was solid in my reasoning, what was weak, and what I never managed to adequately defend.

The position I want to defend:
[STATE YOUR POSITION CLEARLY — one or two sentences]

Start with the most fundamental question you can ask about this position.
```

---

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of This

**Don't be defensive.** When a question exposes a gap, sit with it. Say "I'm not sure" if you're not sure — then think through it.

**Don't deflect.** If you change the subject when a question gets hard, you're doing it wrong.

**Take breaks.** If you get stuck, pause, think for a few minutes without the AI, then come back.

**Track your reasoning.** Write down your main arguments as you make them. At the end, you'll see which held up and which collapsed.

## When to Use This Regularly

This prompt is most valuable when you:
- Are about to make an important decision
- Find yourself very certain about something and want to test that certainty
- Are preparing to write or argue for a position publicly
- Notice you're dismissing a counterargument too quickly
