# Prompt: Identify Hidden Assumptions

**Purpose:** Train your ability to see what arguments take for granted — the invisible load-bearing walls of any claim.

**When to use:** After completing the self-assessment. Repeat with different texts until assumption-spotting becomes automatic.

**Expected duration:** 30–45 minutes per session

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm practicing identifying hidden assumptions in arguments. Your job is to coach me, not do it for me.

Here's how this works:
1. I'll give you a claim or short argument.
2. Ask me probing questions to help me discover the assumptions myself — don't list them for me.
3. If I miss an important assumption, give me a hint (a question that points toward it), not the answer.
4. After I've found the main assumptions, ask me: "Which of these assumptions, if false, would completely destroy the argument?"
5. Finally, give me feedback on what I found well and what I missed, and why those missed ones matter.

The claim I want to analyze:

[PASTE YOUR CLAIM OR ARGUMENT HERE]

Start by asking me what I notice about this claim before we dig into assumptions.
```

---

## Example Claims to Practice With

Start with these, then find your own:

**Beginner:**
> "Students who use laptops in class get worse grades, so schools should ban laptops."

**Intermediate:**
> "Country X has lower crime rates than Country Y, therefore Country X's criminal justice approach is superior."

**Advanced:**
> "Remote work increases productivity, so companies that require office attendance are making a business mistake."

## Signs You're Improving

- You start noticing assumptions in conversations without trying
- You find yourself asking "wait, that assumes X is true — is it?" in daily life
- You're faster at finding the *load-bearing* assumption (not just surface ones)

## Signs You Need More Practice

- You can only find obvious, stated assumptions
- You need the AI to give hints for most assumptions
- You struggle to identify which assumption matters most
