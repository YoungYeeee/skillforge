# Prompt: Steelmanning Practice

**Purpose:** Build the ability to represent opposing views at their strongest — a prerequisite for genuine intellectual honesty and persuasive reasoning.

**When to use:** After completing assumption identification. Return to this when you encounter a belief you strongly disagree with.

**Expected duration:** 30–60 minutes per session

---

## Background

A **strawman** misrepresents an opposing view to make it easier to knock down.
A **steelman** represents an opposing view at its strongest — often stronger than its actual proponents state it.

Steelmanning is hard because it requires you to temporarily inhabit a worldview you reject. It's also one of the most valuable thinking skills you can have: you can't effectively disagree with a position you don't understand.

---

## The Prompt

```
I'm practicing steelmanning — representing opposing views at their strongest form.

My role: I'll share a position I disagree with and attempt to steelman it.
Your role: Push me to make the steelman stronger. Don't accept a weak version.

Here's how to coach me:
1. After I give my steelman attempt, rate it on a scale of 1–5 (1 = strawman, 5 = better than most proponents)
2. Ask me: "Would someone who actually holds this view feel accurately represented?" If not, where does my version fall short?
3. Help me find the strongest evidence, strongest moral intuitions, and strongest reasoning that supports the position — by asking me questions, not by telling me.
4. Keep pushing until my steelman reaches at least a 4/5.

The position I disagree with:
[WRITE THE POSITION HERE — be specific, not vague]

My initial steelman attempt:
[WRITE YOUR ATTEMPT HERE — don't look it up, start from your own understanding]
```

---

## Positions to Practice With

Pick something you have a genuine opinion about:

- A political policy you oppose
- A management style you dislike
- A dietary/lifestyle choice you think is wrong
- A technology you think is harmful
- A historical decision you think was a mistake

**Tip:** The stronger your emotional reaction, the more valuable the exercise. Steelmanning things you mildly disagree with is easy. The real training happens at the edge of your tolerance.

## The Fairness Test

After writing your steelman, find someone who actually holds the opposing view (or use this prompt):

```
Here is my attempt to represent a view I disagree with. Please tell me:
1. Does this accurately represent your position?
2. What's missing or misrepresented?
3. What's the strongest argument for your position that I didn't include?

My steelman: [paste your steelman]
```

If they confirm it's accurate — or if the AI role-playing as a proponent would accept it — you've passed.
