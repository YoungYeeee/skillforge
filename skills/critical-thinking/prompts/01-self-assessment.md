# Prompt: Self-Assessment — Critical Thinking

**Purpose:** Establish your baseline before starting. This helps the AI calibrate its coaching to your actual level.

**When to use:** Day 1, before any other prompts in this module.

**Expected duration:** 20–30 minutes

---

## The Prompt

```
I want to assess my current level in critical thinking so I can learn it more effectively.

Please conduct a structured self-assessment with me. Do NOT lecture me or teach me yet — just assess.

Here's how I'd like you to do it:

1. Ask me one question at a time. Wait for my answer before asking the next.
2. Start with a broad question, then probe deeper based on my responses.
3. Focus on these four areas:
   - Assumption detection: Can I spot what an argument takes for granted?
   - Steelmanning: Can I represent opposing views fairly?
   - Causal reasoning: Do I confuse correlation with causation?
   - Self-awareness: Do I notice my own biases?
4. After 6–8 questions, give me an honest assessment of:
   - What I clearly understand
   - Where my understanding has gaps or blind spots
   - What level I'm at (novice / developing / proficient) in each area
   - Which area I should focus on first

Start with this question: "Tell me about a time you changed your mind about something important. What made you change it?"

If I can't think of an example, ask: "Tell me what 'critical thinking' means to you in your own words, and describe what it looks like when someone does it well."

Remember: your job right now is to understand where I am, not to teach me.
```

## Notes for Learners

- Be honest. An inflated self-assessment will cause the AI to pitch learning at the wrong level.
- If the AI starts teaching before completing the assessment, remind it: "Please finish the assessment first."
- Save this assessment. You'll compare it to your exit assessment when you graduate.
