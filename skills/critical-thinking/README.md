# Critical Thinking

> Evaluate claims, expose hidden assumptions, and reason your way to better conclusions.

**Difficulty:** Beginner → Intermediate
**Estimated Time:** 3–5 hours over 2–3 weeks
**Languages:** English · 中文

---

## Learning Objectives

After completing this module, you will be able to:

- [ ] Identify the explicit and hidden assumptions in any argument
- [ ] Steelman an opposing view (represent it more fairly than its proponents do)
- [ ] Distinguish between correlation and causation in everyday claims
- [ ] Ask questions that expose weak reasoning without needing AI assistance
- [ ] Recognize common cognitive biases in your own thinking

---

## Prerequisites

None. This is a foundational module.

---

## Why This Skill Matters

Most people never examine why they believe what they believe. We absorb beliefs from media, social circles, and emotions, then retrofit reasons afterward. Critical thinking is the practice of reversing that — starting with evidence and reasoning your way to conclusions.

This skill is foundational to everything else: better writing, better decisions, better technical work. Without it, you can be articulate but systematically wrong.

AI makes this both easier and more dangerous. Easier, because you can now get a well-reasoned counterargument to any position in seconds. More dangerous, because AI can generate confident-sounding nonsense that feels like reasoning. Critical thinking helps you use AI as a tool rather than an oracle.

## Self-Assessment Rubric

| Dimension | Novice (1) | Developing (2) | Proficient (3) | Expert (4) |
|-----------|------------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| **Assumption detection** | Can't identify assumptions without help | Finds obvious assumptions with prompting | Regularly spots hidden assumptions in arguments | Automatically questions foundational assumptions others miss |
| **Steelmanning** | Strawmans opposing views | Can represent opposing view accurately | Represents opposing view more charitably than most proponents | Can strengthen opposing arguments beyond how they were originally stated |
| **Causal reasoning** | Frequently confuses correlation with causation | Recognizes the distinction when prompted | Consistently applies the distinction independently | Identifies subtle confounds and alternative explanations |
| **Self-awareness** | Unaware of own biases | Acknowledges biases exist but can't spot them | Catches own biases during reflection | Notices biases as they form in real time |

## Graduation Conditions

You have mastered this module when you can:

- [ ] Read a news article and list 3+ hidden assumptions without AI help
- [ ] Write a steelman of a position you strongly disagree with, and have someone else confirm it's fair
- [ ] Describe a belief you changed your mind about and explain the reasoning that changed it
- [ ] Catch yourself making an assumption in real-time conversation and correct it

**The graduation test:** Find an article arguing for a position you instinctively dislike. Write a 200-word steelman of that position. Then write 200 words critiquing it. Do this without any prompts. If you can do this and your steelman passes the "fairness check" (someone who holds that view would agree it's an accurate representation), you've graduated.
