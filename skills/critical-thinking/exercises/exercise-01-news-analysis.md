# Exercise 01: News Analysis

**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Time:** 20–30 minutes
**AI assistance:** Allowed for feedback only (not during the analysis)

---

## Instructions

This exercise is done **without AI first**. Use AI only at the end to check your work.

**Step 1:** Find a news article or opinion piece published in the last week. Pick something you have a mild opinion about — not something that enrages you (too hard) or bores you (too easy).

**Step 2:** Read it once for comprehension.

**Step 3:** Read it again and complete the worksheet below on your own. No AI yet.

**Step 4:** Use the feedback prompt at the bottom to compare your analysis to what an AI finds.

## Feedback Prompt (Use After Completing the Worksheet)

```

Article summary: [paste 2–3 sentence summary of the article]

My analysis:
- Main claim: [your answer]
- Evidence I identified: [your answers]
- Hidden assumptions I found: [your answers]
- Most load-bearing assumption: [your answer]

Please:
1. Tell me what I found well — assumptions I correctly identified
2. Tell me what I missed — assumptions I didn't catch and why they matter
3. Rate my assumption-finding: novice / developing / proficient
4. Give me one specific thing to look for next time I do this exercise
```

---

## What "Good" Looks Like

A strong analysis will:
- Find at least 3–4 distinct assumptions (not just restatements of the claim)
- Correctly identify the most important assumption (not just the most obvious one)
- Recognize assumptions about the reader's values, not just factual assumptions
- Ask a question that would actually challenge the argument, not just request more information

---

## Do This Exercise Again When

You can find 4+ assumptions quickly and correctly identify the load-bearing one without needing the feedback prompt to tell you what you missed. At that point, move to Exercise 02.
